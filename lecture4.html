Agile Project Management

An approach to project and team management that embodies "Agility" and based on the agile manifesto.

  • The manifesto is a collection of 4 values + 12 principles that define the mindset that all agile teams should strive for.
  • The agile values and principles inform the Why, How and What of agile project management planning and process.

VUCA Parameters and Agile Mindset

VUCA defines the conditions that affect organizations in a changing and complex world.

V - Volatility

The rate of change in a business or situation or major and abrupt change to business plans

U - Uncertainty

The lack of predictability or high potential for surprise or difficult to create concrete future plans

C - Complexity

The high number of interrelated forces, issues, organizations, and factors that would influence the project

A - Ambiguity

The possibility of misunderstanding the conditions and root causes of events or circumstances

ðŸ’¡ Key Insight: If your project has high levels of VUCA parameters, it's a good sign you should consider an Agile approach. Agile works best in industries or projects that are susceptible to or that encourage change and uncertainty.

Scrum

Agile-lightweight process for managing and controlling software and product development in rapidly changing environments.

Scrum Terminology

Product Backlog

  • The central list of all possible ideas, features, tasks, or deliverables for the product
  • Items are prioritized based on importance and value
  • The Product Backlog is constantly evolving and never complete
ðŸ’¡ Think of it as a "to-do list for the whole product."

Sprint Backlog

  • A list of everything that the team commits to achieve in a given Sprint
  • Once created, no one can add to the Sprint Backlog except the development team
ðŸ’¡ Think of it as the "to-do list for this sprint only."

Sprint

  • A time-boxed iteration in Scrum where work is done
  • Sprints can be as short as one week or as long as one month
  • Sprints happen one right after the other, with no breaks
  • Each sprint begins with a plan and ends with a review

Daily Scrum

  • A meeting of 15 or fewer minutes every day of the sprint

Reasons for Scrum Popularity

1

Clear roles & team focus: Clear roles and responsibilities, while continuously emphasizing the power of the team as a whole

2

Regular & predictable meetings/deliveries: Regular and predictable meeting and delivery schedules, formats, and outcomes

3

Supports Agile values & adds structure: Supports and reinforces the Agile values and principles

4

Free, open, lots of guidance/training: Free and open for all to use with huge amounts of online guidance and support

When to Use/Avoid Scrum

âœ“ When to Use Scrum

  • Requirements are not clearly defined
  • Work is delivered in increments
  • Work is measured and controlled
  • Productivity is maximized by applying known technologies
  • Organizations are willing to do anything for project success
  • Project is important and no existing approach will work
  • Control and management is Empirical

âœ— When to Avoid Scrum

  • There isn't a flexible environment
  • Corporate culture isn't conducive to this development environment
  • Teams of developers are more than 10 (six is ideal)
  • Cost is a major issue
  • No management support
  • No formal training available
